# Level 5 — Expert

**Focus:** Setting observability and cloud standards for the organisation and mentoring teams through complex production scenarios.

## Which of these scenarios can you handle confidently today?

- When teams across the organisation are instrumenting their services inconsistently (different log formats, different metric names, different trace sampling strategies), I can define the standard, build the shared libraries or configuration templates, and drive adoption without making it feel like bureaucracy.
  `OTEL SDK configuration` `shared logging library` `standard metric naming` `otel-collector config` `Spring Boot starter` `trace sampling policy` `instrumentation standard` `adoption strategy`

- When a major incident spans multiple teams and the on-call rotation isn't enough to coordinate the response, I can take incident commander role: coordinate the investigation, manage communication to stakeholders, ensure hypotheses are tested systematically, and drive the post-mortem.
  `incident command` `incident timeline` `stakeholder communication` `hypothesis-driven diagnosis` `post-mortem` `blameless retrospective` `incident channel` `action items`

- When the organisation is evaluating whether to adopt a new observability tool (a new APM, a new logging backend, a new tracing platform), I can lead the evaluation: define the criteria, prototype the integration, and produce an honest comparison against what we already have.
  `tool evaluation criteria` `APM comparison` `Grafana vs Cloud Monitoring` `OTEL backend compatibility` `POC scope` `total cost of ownership` `migration path` `vendor lock-in assessment`

- When GKE platform costs are a concern at the leadership level, I can provide the analysis: break down cost by workload, identify optimisation opportunities (spot instances, vertical pod autoscaling, node pool rightsizing), and model the savings and risks of each option.
  `GKE cost by namespace` `Spot VMs` `Vertical Pod Autoscaler (VPA)` `node pool rightsizing` `committed use discounts` `GKE cost allocation` `workload identity` `cost modelling`

- When teams don't know how to write good runbooks, I can define the standard: what a runbook must contain, how to test it, and how to keep it up to date — and run a session where teams actually write and peer-review their runbooks rather than just hearing about it.
  `runbook structure` `alert-to-runbook linking` `runbook testing` `escalation matrix` `diagnostic steps` `runbook review process` `on-call readiness` `alert annotation`

## Role in Training Program

Engineers at this level serve as **Mentors & Workshop Leads** for Track A and Track B.
