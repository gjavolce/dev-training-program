# Level 5 — Expert

**Focus:** Setting architectural standards for the organisation and leading the highest-complexity design work.

## Which of these scenarios can you handle confidently today?

- When multiple teams are making independent architectural decisions that are slowly diverging in ways that will create integration pain, I can identify the pattern, propose the standard, write the ADR, and get enough buy-in that teams actually follow it — without having to mandate it top-down.
  `ADR` `architectural standard` `divergence` `design guild` `RFC process` `buy-in` `Conway's Law` `org-wide pattern`
- When the organisation is considering a major architectural shift (e.g. moving from a monolith to event-driven microservices, or adopting a new data platform), I can scope the work, identify the risks, define what success looks like, and present a phased approach to leadership.
  `strangler fig` `event-driven architecture` `migration strategy` `phased rollout` `risk assessment` `architectural runway` `monolith decomposition` `bounded context mapping`
- When a team is stuck on a design problem that has no clear right answer, I can facilitate the decision process: surface the real trade-offs, challenge hidden assumptions, help the team separate "what we prefer" from "what the constraints require", and get to a decision that the team owns.
  `facilitation` `trade-off analysis` `assumption mapping` `design workshop` `decision criteria` `constraint vs preference` `ADR` `architectural fitness function`
- When a new technology is proposed for adoption (a new messaging system, a new service mesh, a new API gateway), I can lead the evaluation: define the criteria, prototype the relevant scenarios, and produce an honest assessment of fit for our specific context.
  `technology evaluation` `proof of concept` `fit assessment` `service mesh` `API gateway` `Kafka vs alternatives` `non-functional requirements` `vendor lock-in`
- When the training programme needs to cover solution design, I can define the curriculum: what scenarios to cover, what exercises produce real artefacts, and how to structure the progression from "can read a design" to "can lead a design".
  `curriculum design` `scenario-based learning` `design artefact` `ADR writing` `progression framework` `workshop facilitation` `competency mapping`

## Role in Training Program

Engineers at this level serve as **Mentors & Workshop Leads** for Track A and Track B.
