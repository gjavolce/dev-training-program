# Level 6 — Authority

**Focus:** JVM platform strategy, tooling investment, and organisation-wide runtime governance.

## Which of these scenarios can you handle confidently today?

- When the organisation needs to decide on a long-term JVM strategy (LTS versions, GraalVM native, container-native tuning), I can lead the decision: gather input from teams, evaluate options against our actual constraints (startup time, memory footprint, tooling ecosystem), and produce a recommendation at the leadership level.
  `GraalVM native image` `Java LTS` `Project Leyden` `startup time` `memory footprint` `container-native` `AOT compilation` `GKE`

- When we need to build or select tooling to standardise JVM observability across all services (custom Grafana dashboards, shared JFR event libraries, baseline alert rules), I can own the design and adoption — ensuring the tooling works for the range of services we run, not just the ones I know best.
  `JFR custom events` `Micrometer` `Grafana dashboards` `alert rules` `OpenTelemetry` `jvm.memory.used` `platform observability` `shared libraries`

- When a critical production incident involves JVM behaviour at a scale or complexity that stumps the team, I can take ownership of the investigation: organise the evidence, form and test hypotheses, coordinate with engineers across teams, and produce a post-mortem that prevents recurrence.
  `post-mortem` `heap dump` `JFR` `thread dump` `incident command` `root cause analysis` `async-profiler` `cross-team coordination`

- When leadership asks about the cost and risk of migrating the entire backend from Java 17 to Java 21 (or any equivalent platform shift), I can produce a structured assessment: compatibility risks, testing strategy, rollout order, and what "done" looks like.
  `Java 17` `Java 21` `virtual threads` `sequenced collections` `Spring Boot 3` `migration risk` `compatibility matrix` `rollout strategy`

- When there is no structured training track for JVM topics and teams are learning inconsistently, I can identify the gaps, commission or build the curriculum, and ensure the training programme covers what engineers actually need — not just what's interesting to experts.
  `curriculum design` `skills gap` `training programme` `adaptive quiz` `JVM fundamentals` `GC tuning` `observability` `competency levels`
